# Fraxinus Field Mapper

**Fraxinus Field Mapper** is a mobile-friendly web app for capturing and managing location data during environmental fieldwork — no internet required once loaded.

## What it does

You open the app on your phone or tablet in the field, and it lets you record the location of things you find — trees, streams, wetlands, invasive species, sample sites, and more. Each item you record gets GPS coordinates, a label, and any notes you want to add. You can also sketch shapes (lines and areas) directly on the map by hand.

When you're back at your desk, you can export your collected data as standard GIS files (GeoJSON, Shapefile, KML, CSV) and share them with colleagues, or upload them directly to a Felt map for online collaboration.

## Key features

- **GPS capture** — tap to record a point at your current location, or let the app trace your path as you walk a line or boundary
- **Sketch tools** — draw points, lines, and polygons freehand on the map when GPS isn't the right tool
- **Works offline** — everything is saved locally in your browser; no signal needed in the field
- **Import reference layers** — bring in GeoJSON, KML, Shapefiles, GPX tracks, or online map services (WMS, ESRI REST, etc.) as background reference layers
- **Multiple basemaps** — switch between satellite imagery, topographic maps, and OpenStreetMap
- **Felt integration** — upload your collected data directly to a Felt.com map with one tap
- **Export anywhere** — download your data as Shapefile, GeoJSON, KML, or CSV
- **Customisable presets** — configure feature types, colours, and quick-entry shortcuts for your specific survey workflow
- **Grid overlay** — display a configurable grid with area calculations in hectares to help with survey coverage
- **GeoPDF viewer** — view and reference georeferenced PDF maps alongside your collected data

## Who it's for

Environmental field surveyors, ecologists, land managers, and conservation teams who need a lightweight, offline-capable tool for capturing spatial data — without carrying specialist GPS hardware or requiring a data connection in remote areas.
